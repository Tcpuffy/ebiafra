## The Biafran War (1967-1970)

The Nigerian-Biafran War, also known as the Biafran War, was a devastating civil conflict that shaped modern African history.

### Timeline of Major Events

**1966-1967: Pre-War Period**
- Military coups and counter-coups destabilized Nigeria
- Ethnic tensions escalated, particularly between the Igbo and Hausa-Fulani groups
- Pogroms against Igbo residents in northern Nigeria

**May 30, 1967: Declaration of Independence**
- Eastern Region secedes and declares the Republic of Biafra
- Led by Lieutenant Colonel Chukwuemeka Ojukwu

**July 6, 1967 - January 15, 1970: The War**
- Federal forces battle Biafran forces
- Intense military campaigns and territorial changes
- Economic blockade of Biafra

### Human Cost

The war resulted in:
- Estimated 500,000 to 3 million deaths
- Widespread famine and humanitarian crisis
- Massive displacement of populations
- International humanitarian response efforts

### Key Figures

- **Chukwuemeka Ojukwu** - Leader of Biafra
- **Yakubu Gowon** - Head of Federal Military Government
- Various international mediators and supporters

### International Involvement

Several countries provided support to either side:
- France supported Biafra
- Britain supported Nigeria
- The UN was involved in peace efforts

### Outcome and Legacy

- Biafran surrender on January 15, 1970
- Reunification of Nigeria
- Lessons learned about conflict resolution and humanitarian intervention
- Lasting impact on Nigerian identity and regional relations
